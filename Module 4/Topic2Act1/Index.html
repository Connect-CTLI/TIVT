Accommodations & Concessions

Where Additional Staff or Equipment Is Required

Instructions: Tap each card below to reveal the procedures that apply.

Computers Must Be Prepared By Ensuring

  • Spell checkers and other supporting programs are disabled.
  • There is no access to the internet.
  • There is no access to other programs on the computer.
  • Computers must be stand-alone, not networked.
  • Candidate profiles must be created on the computer.
  • After the examination, the candidate's answers must be printed and stapled into the answer book.
  • Devices must be fully charged, and additional equipment must be available in the event of a malfunction.

Separate Venue Requirements

  • The candidate and scribe must be accompanied by an invigilator. A recording must be made of the session.
  • The recording must be retained by the school in the safe or strong room.
  • Schools must ensure that scripts of learners in separate venues are batched together with the rest of the scripts.
  • In the event of an irregularity, investigators may request this recording.

Invigilator

  • Ensure the question is read verbatim, clearly and audibly to the candidate, and that the candidate's answers are recorded verbatim.
  • Ensure the candidate is not provided with answers and that no question is explained.
  • The invigilator may not interact with the candidate.

Scribe / Reader

  • Must be trained by the district and have a certificate in their possession during examinations.
  • School principals or centre managers must meet all scribes before the examination to verify district training.
  • The scribe reads questions to the candidate and writes down spoken answers verbatim.
  • In the case of Deaf candidates, the scribe writes down answers given in Sign Language.
  • The scribe must remain neutral, impartial, and must not become impatient or hurried.
  • The scribe or reader must not serve as an invigilator at the same time, an invigilator must be separately present.
