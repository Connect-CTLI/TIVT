You would have noticed the asterisk (*) next to the word "examinations" at the end of the last two sanctions. What does examination barring mean?

There are two examinations in a year: the May/June (for National Senior Certificate (NSC) and Senior Certificate (SC) candidates) and the October/November examinations (for National Senior Certificate (NSC) candidates). When a candidate is found guilty of an irregularity and is barred from writing that subject in 0 examinations, this means he/she may write that subject in the next examination.
